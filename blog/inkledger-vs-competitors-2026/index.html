1. inkLedger Tattoo Booking vs Venue Ink

What Venue Ink Is

Venue Ink is a booking platform for tattoo artists that bundles your booking form, scheduling, deposits, and client messaging into one place. It is US-based and has both free and paid tiers. Available on iOS, Android, and web.

Key Features

  • Built-in calendar with appointment, deposit, and blocked-time tracking
  • Consolidated client conversations (replaces DM chaos)
  • Flash design gallery with direct purchasing
  • Google Calendar integration to prevent double bookings
  • Multiple booking books for guesting or different services
  • Automated reminders and client profiles

Pricing

  • Solo (Free): 10% booking fee charged to clients on every transaction
  • Studio (Free): Same 10% fee, adds shared calendar and team accounts
  • Solo Pro ($50/month): Drops fee to 5%, adds payment terminal option and monthly reporting
  • Studio Pro ($150/month): 5% fee, auto-split payments, up to 5 artists (+$35/month each), free terminal

Where It Falls Short vs inkLedger

The 10% client-facing fee on the free tier is steep. inkLedger charges an 8% client fee on deposits — lower, and the artist pays nothing. Venue's paid tiers ($50-$150/month) still charge 5% on top of the subscription. With inkLedger, there is no subscription at all. Free for artists, period.

There is no built-in bookkeeping or tax tools. No VAT/BTW splitting. No design approval workflow with magic links. No GDPR-compliant EU data hosting. And no deposit + remaining balance flow through Stripe Checkout.

2. inkLedger Tattoo Booking vs Porter Tattoo Software
